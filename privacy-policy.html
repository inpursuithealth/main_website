1. Overview

InPursuit Health, Inc. ("InPursuit Health," "we," "us," or "our") is a health technology company that builds data orchestration infrastructure for value-based care. We are committed to protecting the privacy and security of personal information, including protected health information (PHI), in compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA), the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health (HITECH) Act, and applicable state privacy laws.

This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard information obtained through our websites (including InPursuitHealth.com), the TETRA™ platform, the TETRA Ex™ health data exchange, TETRA Conductor™, TETRA Aegis™, TETRA Sentinel™, mobile applications, and any other products or services we offer (collectively, the "Services").

By accessing or using our Services, you agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use our Services.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide to Us

  • Account information: Name, email address, phone number, professional credentials (for providers), National Provider Identifier (NPI), and organizational affiliation.
  • Contact and inquiry data: Information you submit through contact forms, email, or when you request a demo or partnership.
  • Payment information: Billing address and payment card details (processed securely through our PCI-DSS compliant payment processor; we do not store raw card data).
  • Communications: Records of your correspondence with us, including support tickets and feedback.

2.2 Protected Health Information (PHI)

If you are a healthcare provider, health plan, or other HIPAA-covered entity using our platform, or if you are a patient whose data flows through the TETRA infrastructure, we may handle Protected Health Information as a Business Associate. See Section 4 for details on how we handle PHI.

2.3 Automatically Collected Information

  • Log data: IP address, browser type, operating system, referring URLs, pages viewed, and timestamps.
  • Device data: Device identifiers, hardware model, and network information.
  • Usage data: Features accessed, queries run, and interaction patterns within the platform.
  • Cookies and similar technologies: See Section 10 for details.

2.4 Information from Third Parties

  • Data received through health information exchanges and interoperability connections you authorize (e.g., FHIR endpoints, HL7 interfaces).
  • Identity verification and professional licensure data from third-party verification services.
  • Publicly available information for business development and compliance purposes.

3. How We Use Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Providing and improving the Services: Operating, maintaining, and enhancing the TETRA platform and related products.
  • Account management: Creating and managing your account, verifying identity, and authenticating access.
  • Communications: Sending transactional emails, service updates, security alerts, and, with your consent, marketing communications.
  • Analytics and research: Analyzing de-identified usage patterns to improve platform performance, user experience, and clinical outcomes. We do not use PHI for commercial analytics without a valid authorization.
  • Compliance and legal obligations: Fulfilling HIPAA, HITECH, and applicable state law requirements; responding to lawful government requests; and enforcing our agreements.
  • Security and fraud prevention: Detecting, investigating, and preventing unauthorized access, security incidents, and fraudulent activity.
  • Business operations: Processing payments, providing customer support, and conducting internal audits.
Our Data Oath Commitment: We will never sell your personal data or PHI to third parties. We will never use your health data to train artificial intelligence or machine learning models without your explicit written consent. These are unconditional commitments. See our full Data Oath.

4. HIPAA & Protected Health Information

4.1 Business Associate Relationship

When InPursuit Health handles PHI on behalf of a HIPAA-covered entity (such as a hospital, clinic, health plan, or clearinghouse), we function as a Business Associate as defined under 45 C.F.R. § 160.103. In this capacity, we are required to:

  • Use or disclose PHI only as permitted or required by the applicable Business Associate Agreement (BAA) and the HIPAA Privacy Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 164).
  • Implement safeguards in accordance with the HIPAA Security Rule (45 C.F.R. §§ 164.302–318).
  • Report breaches of unsecured PHI in compliance with the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule (45 C.F.R. §§ 164.400–414).
  • Provide individuals with access to their PHI upon request through the applicable covered entity.

4.2 Permitted Uses and Disclosures of PHI

As a Business Associate, we may use or disclose PHI only: (a) as directed by the covered entity in the BAA; (b) as required for the proper management and administration of our business; (c) to carry out our legal responsibilities; or (d) as otherwise permitted by HIPAA. We do not use PHI for marketing, advertising, or commercial data analytics without a valid HIPAA authorization.

4.3 Minimum Necessary Standard

We apply the HIPAA minimum necessary standard, limiting access to PHI to those workforce members and systems that need it to perform their job functions. Role-based access controls are enforced across all TETRA platform components.

4.4 Breach Notification

In the event of a breach of unsecured PHI, we will notify the relevant covered entity without unreasonable delay and within 60 calendar hours of discovery, in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.410. Covered entities retain responsibility for notifying affected individuals and, where applicable, the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS).

4.5 De-Identification

Data that has been de-identified in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.514(b) (Safe Harbor method) or § 164.514(c) (Expert Determination method) is no longer PHI under HIPAA and may be used for analytics, research, and platform improvement without restriction.

5. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information or PHI. We may share information in the following limited circumstances:

5.1 Service Providers (Subprocessors)

We engage vetted third-party vendors to help us deliver our Services, including cloud infrastructure providers, security monitoring services, and payment processors. These subprocessors are bound by data processing agreements and, where applicable, Business Associate Agreements. They are prohibited from using your data for any purpose other than providing services to InPursuit Health. A current subprocessor list is available upon request at privacy@InPursuitHealth.com.

5.2 Legal Requirements and Safety

We may disclose information if required by law, regulation, judicial or governmental order, or where we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to: (a) comply with a legal obligation; (b) protect the rights, property, or safety of InPursuit Health, our users, or the public; or (c) detect, prevent, or address fraud, security, or technical issues.

5.3 Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, reorganization, or sale of all or substantially all of our assets, personal information may be transferred to the successor entity. We will provide notice prior to any such transfer becoming subject to a materially different privacy policy, and affected individuals will have the opportunity to exercise any applicable rights.

5.4 With Your Consent

We may share your information for any other purpose with your explicit prior consent.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to provide our Services, comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. Specifically:

  • Account data: Retained for the duration of your account plus 7 years after closure (to satisfy HIPAA record retention requirements).
  • PHI: Retained in accordance with the applicable BAA and applicable state law (minimum 6 years from creation or last effective date under 45 C.F.R. § 164.530(j)).
  • Log and audit data: Retained for a minimum of 6 years per HIPAA Security Rule requirements.
  • Anonymized/de-identified data: May be retained indefinitely for analytics and platform improvement.

Upon expiration of the applicable retention period or upon a valid deletion request, we will securely destroy or de-identify information in accordance with NIST SP 800-88 media sanitization guidelines.

7. Security

We implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards designed to protect personal information and PHI against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. Our security program includes:

  • AES-256 encryption at rest and TLS 1.3 encryption in transit for all data
  • Role-based access controls (RBAC) with least-privilege enforcement
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all system access
  • Continuous security monitoring and intrusion detection
  • Annual third-party penetration testing and HIPAA security risk assessments
  • SOC 2 Type II audit program (in progress)
  • Workforce security training and background checks
  • Documented incident response and disaster recovery plans

No security system is impenetrable. If you believe your account has been compromised, contact us immediately at security@InPursuitHealth.com.

8.2 Correction

8.3 Deletion

You may request deletion of your personal information, subject to our legal retention obligations. Note that we may be required to retain certain information for compliance purposes even after account closure.

8.4 Restriction and Objection

You may request that we restrict processing of your personal information, or object to certain uses, where permitted by applicable law.

8.5 Withdrawal of Consent

Where processing is based on your consent, you may withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing prior to withdrawal.

8.6 How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of the above rights, contact our Privacy Officer at privacy@InPursuitHealth.com or by mail at the address in Section 13. We will respond to verified requests within 30 days (or such shorter period as required by applicable law).

9. Children's Privacy

Our Services are not directed to individuals under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected personal information from a child under 18, we will take prompt steps to delete that information. If you believe we have collected information from a child under 18, please contact us immediately at privacy@InPursuitHealth.com.

For minor patients whose PHI flows through our platform, access and authorization are governed by applicable HIPAA rules and state law regarding parental and guardian rights.

10. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies on our websites to:

  • Essential cookies: Maintain session state, authentication, and security. These cannot be disabled without affecting core functionality.
  • Functional cookies: Remember your preferences and settings.
  • Analytics cookies: Understand how visitors interact with our website using privacy-preserving analytics tools. We configure these tools to anonymize IP addresses and disable data sharing with third-party ad networks.

We do not use third-party advertising cookies or behavioral tracking technologies on our clinical platform. You may configure your browser to refuse cookies or to alert you when cookies are being sent. Note that some features of our website may not function properly if cookies are disabled.

11. State-Specific Privacy Rights

California (CCPA/CPRA)

California residents have rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), including the right to know what personal information we collect, the right to delete, the right to correct, the right to opt out of sale or sharing, and the right to limit use of sensitive personal information. We do not sell personal information. California residents may submit rights requests to privacy@InPursuitHealth.com. We will not discriminate against you for exercising these rights.

Texas (TDPSA)

Texas residents have rights under the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA), including rights to access, correct, delete, and obtain a portable copy of personal data, and to opt out of targeted advertising and profiling. Requests may be submitted to our Privacy Officer.

Other States

Residents of Virginia (VCDPA), Colorado (CPA), Connecticut (CTDPA), and other states with comprehensive privacy laws have similar rights. We will honor verified requests in accordance with applicable law. Contact our Privacy Officer to submit a request.
