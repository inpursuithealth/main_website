What This Transformation Requires

A patient-centered healthcare ecosystem cannot run on today's infrastructure. The current system was built for billing, not for patients. Transitioning 140+ million Americans to outcome-based care requires a fundamentally new orchestration layer—one that federates fragmented data across every EHR, payer, pharmacy, lab, and wearable device, then makes it actionable at the point of care. It requires interoperability that actually works, not as a compliance checkbox, but as living infrastructure that moves data in real time across the entire care continuum.

That is exactly what TETRA was built to do. TETRA is an AI-enabled data interoperability and orchestration infrastructure — the connective tissue that makes patient-centered care coordination, value-based payment, consumer health engagement, and clinical AI possible at national scale. It is not one application. It is the infrastructure that every application in this new ecosystem depends on.

TETRA operates as five integrated infrastructure layers. TETRA is the core orchestration engine — federating fragmented data across EHRs, payers, pharmacies, labs, and wearable devices into actionable intelligence at the point of care. TETRA Ex is the health data exchange layer — enabling real-time, bidirectional interoperability across the care continuum through partnerships with Particle Health, b.well Connected Health, and eHealth Exchange. TETRA Conductor™ is the model-agnostic AI orchestration layer — evaluating clinical context in real-time and routing each interaction to the optimal AI model, agent, or capability with zero vendor lock-in. TETRA Aegis™ is the AI governance layer — the autonomous supervisory control plane that ensures AI operates safely within clinical environments. TETRA Sentinel™ is the behavioral surveillance layer — hallucination detection, drift monitoring, and adversarial defense, structurally independent from the models it supervises.

Together, these five layers form the complete infrastructure stack that the patient-centered ecosystem requires. No single competitor offers all five. And as AI becomes central to every clinical and consumer health workflow, the need for governed, interoperable, model-agnostic infrastructure only accelerates.

Could you imagine connecting your personal healthcare data directly to Claude, ChatGPT, or any AI assistant—with no governance layer in between? The potential is enormous. But $7.42 million per healthcare breach, 97% of AI deployments lacking proper access controls, and 20% of organizations already breached through shadow AI make it clear: the opportunities for AI interaction with health data will only grow, and the infrastructure that makes it safe is a trillion-dollar necessity.
