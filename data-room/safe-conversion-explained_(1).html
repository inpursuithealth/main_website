How Conversion Works
Your SAFE Converts at the Lower of Two Prices.
Per Section 1(a) of the SAFE Agreement, when InPursuit raises an Equity Financing of $1,000,000 or more, your SAFE automatically converts into Safe Preferred Units. The number of units you receive equals your Purchase Amount divided by the Conversion Price — which is the lower of the Safe Price or the Liquidity Price.

What It Means in Practice

No matter how high the Equity Financing prices the Company, your SAFE converts as if the Company were worth $25,000,000. The higher the Equity Financing price, the greater your advantage.

Investor Protections
Your SAFE Includes Two Key Protections.
In addition to the $25M valuation cap, your SAFE includes a Most Favored Nation provision and pro rata rights — both detailed in the executed agreements.
Pro Rata Rights

Maintain Your Ownership

If your investment is $100,000 or greater, you have the right (but not the obligation) to participate in the Equity Financing on the same terms as new investors — maintaining your ownership percentage. You must notify the Company within 10 days of the Equity Financing closing. (Subscription Agreement, Section 4)

Most Favored Nation (MFN)

Always Get the Best Terms

If InPursuit issues any future SAFEs or convertible securities with more favorable terms (lower cap, added discount, better economics), you can elect to adopt those terms within 10 days of notice. Your SAFE is automatically amended to match. (SAFE Agreement, Section 6)

🛡

What the MFN means in practice: If InPursuit later offers a SAFE with a $20M cap or a 20% discount, you automatically get those better terms if you choose. You can never be disadvantaged by being early. The MFN ensures the earliest investors always have access to the best deal the Company offers to anyone.

What Happens at Exit
Liquidation Priority: Where You Stand.
Per Section 1(d) of the SAFE Agreement, in a Liquidity Event (acquisition, IPO) or Dissolution Event, your claim is structured as follows:

Liquidation Priority (SAFE Agreement, Section 1(d))

First PriorityOutstanding indebtedness and creditor claims
Second Priority (You)SAFE holders and convertible notes (on par)
Third PriorityCommon Units (Class A members)

In a Liquidity Event (Section 1(b)), you receive the greater of your Purchase Amount (cash-out) or the conversion value at the Liquidity Price. In a Dissolution Event (Section 1(c)), you receive your Purchase Amount. In both cases, SAFE holders are senior to Common Unit holders — meaning founders and Class A members are paid after you.
