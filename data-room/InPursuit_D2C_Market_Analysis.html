Why This Market Has Waited for InPursuit
1
The Infrastructure Didn't Exist
Connecting consumer wellness data to clinical EHRs in real-time requires healthcare data exchange infrastructure that most companies cannot build. TETRA's five-layer stack — built for DoD/VA at 41K+ server scale — makes this possible. Consumer health companies built apps without the infrastructure underneath.
2
Distribution Was Always the Blocker
Healthcare consumer apps face brutal acquisition economics. HIPAA-compliant advertising is restrictive. Trust is hard to earn. The only shortcut is institutional credibility — and InPursuit has the federal government's endorsement via the CMS Medicare App Library. No competitor has this distribution advantage.
3
Model-Agnostic AI Changes Everything
Every consumer health AI app today is locked to one model. When that model is wrong, there's no fallback. TETRA Conductor routes each user's query to the optimal AI model for their specific health context — a specialized pharmacogenomics model for medication questions, a population health model for chronic disease, a behavioral AI for mental health. The AI surface that consumers interact with is always best-in-class.
4
The Provider-to-Consumer Flywheel
InPursuit's VBC provider network creates a bidirectional flywheel. Providers enrolled in ACCESS Model fund the free Consumer Connected tier for their patients. Those patients generate health data that flows back into the provider's care coordination. Every new provider adds patients. Every new patient improves outcomes. Outcomes drive higher OAPs. Higher OAPs pay for more patient enrollments.
