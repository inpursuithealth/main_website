SAFE Bridge — $2M at $25M Cap
InPursuit Health is raising a $2M SAFE bridge at a $25M valuation cap with no discount. The SAFE converts to Series A Preferred equity when the Series A closes (target: September 1, 2026). This is the primary fundraising instrument and is execution-ready via Carta.

Key Terms
Converts at Series A close. SAFE holders receive preferred equity at the $25M cap price, regardless of the Series A valuation (target: $50M+ pre-money).
No debt, no interest, no maturity. The SAFE is not a loan. No repayment obligation. Clean and founder-friendly.
Early investor advantage. SAFE investors lock in at $25M while the Series A targets $50M+ — capturing significant upside from the valuation step-up.
Pro rata rights. SAFE holders receive the right to participate in the Series A to maintain their ownership percentage.
120-day bridge to revenue. Funds deploy from close through July 5, 2026 when ACCESS OAP revenue begins flowing from Honeycomb Medical Group.
Tax Advantages — 2 provisions (upon conversion)
The SAFE is a contractual right to future equity — not current ownership. It does not generate K-1 reporting or pass-through benefits. Tax advantages activate when the SAFE converts to preferred stock at the Series A close.
IRC §1202 — QSBS
Up to 100% capital gains exclusion
When the SAFE converts to preferred stock at the Series A, those shares may qualify for QSBS treatment — enabling exclusion of up to 100% of capital gains (up to $10M or 10× cost basis) for shares held 5+ years.

IRC §1244 — Ordinary loss protection
Deduct up to $100K/year as ordinary loss
If the investment results in a loss after conversion, Section 1244 allows investors to deduct up to $50,000 ($100,000 for joint filers) as an ordinary loss.
$100K ordinary loss (joint)
• Available upon C-Corp conversion
Tax benefits activate at Series A conversion. The SAFE itself is not equity or debt and does not generate K-1 pass-through benefits. Consult your tax advisor.

Convertible Promissory Note — $25M Cap
A debt instrument that accrues interest and converts to Series A Preferred equity at the earlier of the Series A close or maturity. Provides additional downside protection through a repayment obligation and interest accrual.

Key Terms
Same $25M cap as the SAFE. Converts at the same price as SAFE holders, maintaining cap table parity. Interest accrual adds incremental equity at conversion.
Legally enforceable debt. Unlike a SAFE, the note creates a repayment obligation at maturity. If the Series A doesn't close, investors have a legal claim to principal + accrued interest.
Interest accrues at 5% annually. Accrued interest converts to additional equity at the Series A, increasing total shares received beyond the principal amount.
18-month maturity. If no qualifying financing occurs, the note matures and principal + interest become due. Provides a contractual backstop that SAFEs do not offer.
Ideal for investors seeking downside protection. Combines the upside of equity conversion with the security of a debt instrument.
Tax Advantages — 3 provisions (upon conversion + debt benefit)
The convertible note is debt until conversion. Tax advantages activate upon conversion at the Series A. The debt structure provides a unique additional protection that neither the SAFE nor priced equity offer.
IRC §1202 — QSBS
Up to 100% capital gains exclusion
When the note converts, shares may qualify for QSBS — up to 100% of capital gains excluded (up to $10M or 10× cost basis) for shares held 5+ years. Accrued interest increases cost basis.
$10M+ exclusion5-year hold
• Available upon C-Corp conversion
IRC §1244 — Ordinary loss protection
Deduct up to $100K/year as ordinary loss
If converted shares result in a loss, deduct up to $50,000 ($100,000 joint) as an ordinary loss against wages and other income.
$100K ordinary loss (joint)
• Available upon C-Corp conversion
IRC §166 — Bad debt deduction (unique to notes)
Full ordinary loss if note becomes worthless
If the note is not converted and becomes wholly worthless, investors may claim a bad debt deduction under §166 — treating the entire loss as an ordinary deduction. Dual-layer downside protection unique to the convertible note.
Full principal as ordinary loss
• Available now — debt instrument
§1202 and §1244 activate at Series A conversion. §166 bad debt deduction is available if the note becomes worthless without converting. Consult your tax advisor.

Key Terms
Immediate equity ownership. No waiting for a conversion event. Units are issued upon closing and reflected in the operating agreement and Carta cap table.
K-1 pass-through tax benefits. As an LLC member, you receive annual K-1 reporting. Your pro-rata share of the company's R&D tax credits and qualified business income flows through to your personal return.
Valuation certainty. Price is fixed at investment, not deferred to a future round. You know exactly what you own from day one.
Participates in Series A. If InPursuit converts to a C-Corp, LLC units convert to preferred stock — unlocking §1202 QSBS and §1244 protections on top of R&D credits and QBI deductions already received.
Ideal for GPs, family offices, and high-net-worth investors. The combination of immediate pass-through benefits and future QSBS eligibility creates the strongest risk-adjusted, tax-optimized position of any instrument offered.
Tax Advantages — 4 provisions (2 available now, 2 upon conversion)
Priced equity is the most tax-advantaged instrument. As an LLC member, you receive two immediate K-1 pass-through benefits. Upon C-Corp conversion, two additional protections unlock. Four IRC provisions total.
IRC §41 — R&D tax credit pass-through
Dollar-for-dollar federal tax reduction
Your pro-rata share of InPursuit's qualified R&D expenditures generates tax credits via K-1. TETRA™ development, TETRA Aegis™ AI governance, and clinical validation are qualifying expenses. Credit rate: up to 20% of QREs over base amount.
Up to 20% creditK-1 pass-through
• Available now — LLC structure
IRC §199A — Qualified business income deduction
Deduct up to 20% of your share of business income
Made permanent by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (July 4, 2025). For a family office with $200K of pass-through income, that is a potential $40,000 deduction. Only available while InPursuit operates as an LLC.
20% QBI deductionPermanent (OBBBA)
• Available now — LLC structure
IRC §1202 — QSBS
Up to 100% capital gains exclusion
Upon C-Corp conversion, up to 100% of capital gains excluded ($10M or 10× cost basis) for shares held 5+ years. Stacks on top of R&D credits and QBI deductions already received.
$10M+ exclusion5-year hold
• Available upon C-Corp conversion
IRC §1244 — Ordinary loss protection
Deduct up to $100K/year as ordinary loss
If the investment results in a loss after conversion, deduct up to $50,000 ($100,000 joint) as an ordinary loss. Combined with benefits already received, creates the strongest risk-adjusted position.
$100K ordinary loss (joint)
• Available upon C-Corp conversion
§41 and §199A are available immediately via K-1. §199A was made permanent by OBBBA (July 4, 2025). §1202 and §1244 activate upon C-Corp conversion. Consult your tax advisor. InPursuit Health does not provide tax advice.
