Company Overview
InPursuit Health builds the infrastructure beneath every payer, every provider, every patient.

InPursuit Health, LLC is a healthcare infrastructure company operating in the CMS Health IT Ecosystem. We build TETRA™—a clinical data interoperability and orchestration layer that connects fragmented provider, payer, pharmacy, lab, and device data into actionable intelligence. TETRA is payer-agnostic, model-agnostic, and EHR-agnostic. It powers both provider-facing value-based care programs and a direct-to-consumer health app available to every American.

InPursuit is infrastructure, not an app. The consumer app and provider tools are outputs of the TETRA stack. AI companies, EHR vendors, payer systems, and consumer applications sit on top of TETRA. When any of these systems need to interact with health data safely, interoperably, and with governance, they depend on our infrastructure.

Revenue Model
Direct Payer Billing. Four Independent Streams.

InPursuit bills payers directly under its own NPI. We do not charge providers a percentage of VBC revenue. Referring providers earn CMS co-management payments (~$100/patient/year/track) paid by CMS independently. Our four revenue streams are independently billable and compound on the same patient base.

Entity Architecture
Two Entities. One Infrastructure.

Phase 1 (now): InPursuit Health, LLC is the operating entity. Provider partners refer patients. InPursuit delivers technology-enabled care and bills payers through partner physician billing arrangements. First revenue July 5, 2026 from Honeycomb Medical Group via ACCESS Model.

Phase 2 (volume-triggered): InPursuit Medical, PC—a clinician-owned professional corporation with a managed services agreement (MSA) with InPursuit Health, LLC—delivers telehealth and virtual care. Owns an organizational NPI for direct billing of RPM, CCM, telehealth, and annual wellness visits without routing through partner physicians. Triggers at ~$500K/month billing volume, ~10+ practices.

Both entities generate independent revenue from the same patient base. The TETRA infrastructure serves both without rebuilding a single layer.

Traction & Validation
Federal Proof Points. First Revenue in Sight.

DoD/VA Validation: The Department of Defense and Veterans Affairs selected TETRA over IBM, Oracle, SAP, and Informatica in a formal Analysis of Alternatives to solve the largest healthcare data interoperability challenge in the federal government. TETRA was deployed on 41,000 servers.

CMS Health IT Ecosystem: InPursuit is an approved member of the CMS Health IT Ecosystem and was selected for all three patient-facing categories in the Medicare App Library launched February 2026—Conversational AI, Kill the Clipboard, and Chronic Care Management.

Honeycomb Medical Group: First signed provider partner. Memphis, Tennessee. ~22,000 patients. Submitting CMS ACCESS Model Cohort 1 application. Revenue begins July 5, 2026.

IP Protection: TETRA Aegis provisional patent filed February 2026 covering the Autonomous Supervisory AI Control Plane across six domains. Six-layer infrastructure stack protected by trade secret and patent.
