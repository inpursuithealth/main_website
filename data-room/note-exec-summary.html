Tax Advantages
Three IRC Provisions. One Available Now.

§166 Bad Debt Deduction (available immediately): As a debt instrument, the Convertible Note qualifies for bad debt deduction under IRC §166 if the investment results in a loss. Business bad debts are deductible as ordinary losses with no annual cap. This provision is not available to SAFE holders and does not require C-Corp conversion.

§1202 QSBS (deferred to C-Corp conversion): Upon conversion to equity at the Series A, investors may exclude up to 100% of capital gains (up to $10M or 10x basis) on shares held 5+ years.

§1244 Stock (deferred to C-Corp conversion): If the investment results in a loss after conversion, investors may deduct up to $50,000 ($100,000 joint) as an ordinary loss rather than a capital loss.

Note: InPursuit Health, LLC is currently taxed as a partnership. C-Corp conversion planned at Series A close (September 1, 2026). Investors should consult their own tax advisor.
