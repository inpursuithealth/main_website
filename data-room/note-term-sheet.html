Why the Note vs. the SAFE? Both instruments offer the same $25M cap and convert at Series A. The Note adds three advantages: (1) 5% interest accrual increases your converting principal, (2) 18-month maturity provides a legally enforceable repayment right if no financing occurs, and (3) an additional tax provision (§166 bad debt deduction) available immediately as a debt instrument.
Tax Advantages
Three IRC Provisions — One Available Now
§166 Bad DebtAvailable immediately. As a debt instrument, the Note qualifies for bad debt deduction under IRC §166 if the investment results in a loss. Business bad debts are deductible as ordinary losses with no annual cap. This provision is not available to SAFE holders.
§1202 QSBSDeferred to C-Corp conversion. Up to 100% capital gains exclusion ($10M or 10x basis) on shares held 5+ years after conversion.
§1244 StockDeferred to C-Corp conversion. Ordinary loss deduction up to $50,000 ($100,000 joint) if investment results in loss after conversion to equity.
Note: InPursuit Health, LLC is currently taxed as a partnership. C-Corp conversion is planned at Series A close (September 1, 2026). §166 is available now; §1202 and §1244 activate upon conversion. Investors should consult their own tax advisor.
