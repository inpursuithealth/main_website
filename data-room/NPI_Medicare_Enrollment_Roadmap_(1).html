Why This Matters

NPI credentialing is the single most important operational milestone in InPursuit's path to revenue.

InPursuit's corrected revenue architecture depends on the company's ability to bill Medicare and commercial payers directly — not through the provider, not as a percentage of provider payments, but independently under InPursuit's own National Provider Identifier (NPI) and Medicare Part B enrollment.

Without NPI and Medicare enrollment, InPursuit cannot:

Bill Medicare directly for RPM, CCM, TCM, BHI, or other care management services (Engine 2b)

Apply as an ACCESS Model Participant under its own TIN for Cohort 2 (Engine 2a)

Credential with commercial payers — most require active Medicare enrollment before they will process an application (Engine 2b, commercial)

Operate the $0-cost provider partnership model — the entire structure depends on InPursuit earning from payers directly rather than charging the provider

Part of the $2M bridge round is explicitly allocated to fund NPI credentialing, Medicare enrollment, commercial payer credentialing, and the clinical infrastructure required to support direct billing.

The Three Sequential Gates

Three gates. Overlapping timelines. One critical path.

The credentialing process involves three sequential-but-overlapping gates. Each has its own timeline, requirements, and dependencies. Managed correctly, the total elapsed time from initiation to first billing is 90-120 days.

Gate 1 — NPI Number
1-10 days

National Provider Identifier (Type 2 — Organizational)

The NPI is a 10-digit unique identifier assigned by CMS to every healthcare entity that bills federal programs. InPursuit requires a Type 2 NPI (organizational), applied for through the National Plan and Provider Enumeration System (NPPES). The online application takes approximately 20 minutes. The NPI is typically issued within 1-10 business days. It is free.

This is the prerequisite for everything else. Medicare enrollment will be rejected without an active NPI. Commercial payer applications require it. It should be filed immediately.

Gate 2 — Medicare Part B Enrollment
30-90 days

CMS-855B via PECOS (Provider Enrollment, Chain, and Ownership System)

After the NPI is issued, InPursuit files the CMS-855B application through PECOS — the online Medicare enrollment portal. This is the application for Clinics/Group Practices and Other Suppliers. PECOS applications process in 30-60 days (paper applications take 90-120 days). The 2026 application fee is $750.

CMS conducts primary source verification of all ownership, licensure, and compliance information. A site visit to InPursuit's physical location may be required. Any deficiency in the application triggers a development letter, which must be responded to within 30 days or the application is withdrawn.

Gate 3 — Commercial Payer Credentialing
45-120 days (parallel)

Major Payer Panel Enrollment — Run Simultaneously with Gate 2

Commercial payer credentialing can begin as soon as the NPI is active, running in parallel with Medicare enrollment. Most commercial payers pull information from CAQH ProView — a centralized credentialing database — which must be set up and attested before any applications are submitted. CAQH attestation must be renewed every 120 days.

Parallel Requirements

What else must be completed alongside credentialing.

Medical Director — Contracted licensed physician for clinical oversight and supervisory authority Required for Medicare enrollment and for signing care management orders. Can be one of Honeycomb's physicians under the oversight agreement, or a dedicated part-time/contracted Medical Director.
Professional Liability Insurance — Malpractice / E&O coverage Required by Medicare and all commercial payers. For a care management organization: $15,000-40,000/year depending on coverage limits and number of clinicians. Must be active before any enrollment application is submitted.
Compliance Program — Written policies, compliance officer, training, reporting mechanism Medicare requires enrolled entities to maintain a compliance program. SOC 2 Type II and HITRUST e1 certification (already on InPursuit's roadmap) will satisfy the majority of these requirements.
Clinical Protocols and Documentation Templates Standardized care plans, escalation protocols, and documentation workflows for each CPT code (99453/99454/99457 for RPM, 99490 for CCM, 99495/99496 for TCM, 99484/99492 for BHI). Must meet payer-specific requirements for billing.
Revenue Cycle Management (RCM) Infrastructure Claims submission system, clearinghouse enrollment, payment posting, eligibility verification, denial management, and AR follow-up. Can be outsourced to an RCM partner initially (4-8% of collections or $3-8 per claim).
State Licensure — Tennessee (initial market) Tennessee business license for healthcare entities. If InPursuit renders telehealth services across state lines as the provider network grows, each additional state requires compliance with that state's telehealth and corporate practice of medicine laws.
Care Management Staff Hiring Minimum staffing: 1 care coordinator per 200-300 RPM/CCM patients, 1 supervising RN per 3-5 coordinators. Initial staff needed for Honeycomb launch: 2-3 care coordinators + 1 RN supervisor.
CAQH ProView Re-Attestation (every 120 days) Set recurring calendar reminder. Lapsed attestation causes immediate rejection of all pending commercial payer applications and resets the timeline.

Critical Dependency

NPI credentialing is the prerequisite for Engines 2a (ACCESS OAPs) and 2b (direct FFS billing). Without it, InPursuit cannot bill any payer directly, cannot apply as an ACCESS Participant, and cannot operate the $0-cost provider partnership model. Every week of delay in filing the NPI application is a week of delay in reaching first revenue from direct payer billing.
