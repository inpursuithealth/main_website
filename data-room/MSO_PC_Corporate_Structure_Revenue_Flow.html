The Structure

Two entities. One business. All revenue flows to InPursuit Health.

Most states prohibit non-physician companies from directly practicing medicine — a legal doctrine called the Corporate Practice of Medicine (CPOM). This doctrine exists to protect clinical independence. It does not prevent technology companies from operating in healthcare. It simply requires a specific corporate structure.

The solution — used by every major virtual care company — is the MSO–PC model: a Management Services Organization (the technology and operations company) paired with a physician-owned Professional Corporation (the clinical compliance entity). The MSO runs the business. The PC provides the clinical governance. The revenue flows through the PC and is consolidated at the MSO.

The MSO — InPursuit Health, LLC

The Business. The Technology. The Revenue.

InPursuit Health, LLC is the operating company. It owns all intellectual property, technology, data infrastructure, brand, consumer app, and non-clinical operations. It is the entity that raises capital, holds investor equity, and generates enterprise value.

InPursuit Health owns and operates:

  • TETRA™ data orchestration infrastructure
  • InPursuit consumer health application
  • All intellectual property and patent-pending claims
  • Non-clinical staff (engineers, sales, operations, data)
  • Payer contracting and revenue cycle management
  • Clinical care coordination staff (non-physician)
  • Marketing, compliance infrastructure, and analytics
  • Provider partnership agreements
The PC — InPursuit Medical, PC

The Clinical Compliance Wrapper.

InPursuit Medical, PC is a physician-owned professional corporation. It is 100% owned by the Medical Director — a licensed physician who provides clinical governance and oversight. The PC exists to comply with CPOM laws. It is not an independent business.

The PC holds:

  • Medical licenses and clinical authority
  • Physician supervision and clinical protocols
  • NPI and Medicare enrollment for clinical billing
  • Contracted or employed rendering clinicians
  • Clinical decision-making authority (independent from MSO)

The PC has NO independent investors, NO separate fundraise, and NO separate strategic direction. It is managed by InPursuit Health under a Management Services Agreement.

The Key Principle

The MSO provides all non-clinical services to the PC under a Management Services Agreement at fair market value. The physician retains full clinical autonomy. The MSO retains operational and financial control of the business. This is not a workaround. This is the legally prescribed structure for technology-enabled healthcare delivery in the United States.

Legal Architecture (Phase 3, If Formed)

Five agreements govern the MSO-PC relationship.

1. Management Services Agreement (MSA)

The cornerstone. Defines all services the MSO provides to the PC and the management fee structure. Fee set at fair market value by independent assessment.

2. Stock Transfer Restriction Agreement (STRA)

Prevents the physician from transferring PC ownership without MSO consent. Requires transfer to an MSO-selected physician if the owner departs. Ensures continuity and alignment.

3. Employment Agreement

The MSO employs the Medical Director (the PC owner). Compensation at fair market value. Creates financial and operational alignment between physician and MSO.

4. Technology Licensing Agreement

MSO licenses TETRA, the consumer app, telehealth and virtual care capabilities, and all IP/trademarks to the PC. Part of the management fee justification.

5. Administrative Services Agreement

Covers credentialing, payer contracting, compliance monitoring, and staffing coordination between MSO and PC.

Federal Validation
