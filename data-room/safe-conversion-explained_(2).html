How It Works
Two Mechanisms. Investor Gets the Better Deal.
A SAFE can include a valuation cap, a discount, or both. InPursuit's SAFE uses a cap only. Here's the difference — and why it doesn't change your return at our target Series A price.
Your SAFE — Cap Only

$25M Valuation Cap

No matter what the Series A prices at, your SAFE converts as if the company were worth $25M. If the Series A is at $50M, you're buying units at half price.

$100K ÷ $25M = 0.40% ownership

At a $50M Series A, your 0.40% is worth $200K on Day 1.

Alternative — Cap + 20% Discount

Better of Cap or Discount

With a discount, the investor converts at the lower of the cap OR 20% off the Series A price. The investor always gets whichever is better for them.

Better of: $25M cap OR 80% × Series A price

At $50M Series A: Cap = $25M, Discount = $40M. Cap wins. Same result.

⇌

The breakeven point is $31.25M. Below that, a 20% discount would give investors a better deal than the cap. Above that, the cap always wins. Since your Series A targets $50M pre-money — well above the breakeven — the discount is irrelevant. Both mechanisms produce the exact same outcome.

The Bottom Line

Cap Only = Simpler. Same Return.

At InPursuit's target $50M pre-money Series A, a $25M cap with no discount and a $25M cap with 20% discount produce the exact same result: 2.0x Day 1 markup. The discount only matters below $31.25M — a scenario nobody is planning for. Your attorney structured it right.
