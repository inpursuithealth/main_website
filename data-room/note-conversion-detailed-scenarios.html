Dual Protection Structure
Equity Upside and Debt Backstop — Simultaneously.
A convertible note provides two independent protections that cannot coexist in a SAFE. Both are active from the moment you invest.
Protection 1 — Equity Path

$25M Cap Conversion

When InPursuit Health closes its Series A — targeted at $50M+ pre-money — your note converts to Preferred equity at the $25M cap. At a $50M Series A, this means you receive shares priced as if the company were worth $25M, giving you twice the ownership as someone investing at the $50M round price. Your upside is unbounded as the company grows post-Series A.

Protection 2 — Debt Backstop

18-Month Maturity Repayment

If no qualifying Series A closes within 18 months, your note becomes a legal debt obligation. The company is contractually required to repay $107,500 ($100K principal + $7,500 interest at maturity). This legal claim cannot be restructured away and is enforceable in any jurisdiction. SAFE investors have no equivalent right — they hold equity warrants with no repayment guarantee.

How the backstop works: If InPursuit Health has not closed a qualifying Series A financing by the 18-month maturity date, the full principal plus accrued simple interest ($107,500) becomes immediately due and payable.   • Typically slightly easier to execute than a note

The note is strictly superior in protection — at the same conversion price.

Tax Considerations
Three IRC Provisions Working in Your Favor.
Convertible notes issued by qualified small businesses may be structured to take advantage of favorable federal tax treatment. Consult your tax advisor regarding applicability to your specific situation.
IRC § 1202
Qualified Small Business Stock
Upon conversion, shares may qualify for QSBS treatment — up to 100% federal capital gains exclusion on the first $10M of gain per taxpayer (or 10x invested capital) for shares held 5+ years in a qualified C-corp. Requires C-corp conversion at or before Series A.
IRC § 1244
Ordinary Loss Treatment
If the investment results in a loss, IRC § 1244 allows individual investors to deduct up to $50,000 ($100,000 joint) as ordinary income loss rather than capital loss — providing more immediate tax benefit. Applies to stock in qualified small businesses.
IRC § 1045
Rollover of QSBS Gains
If shares are sold before the 5-year QSBS holding period, gain may be rolled into another QSBS investment within 60 days without immediate recognition — preserving tax deferral and allowing redeployment of capital into the next opportunity.
