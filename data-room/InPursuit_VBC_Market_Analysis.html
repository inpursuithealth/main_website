They cannot afford $100K–$200K infrastructure buildouts. They have no IT departments. They need a partner who deploys everything, handles all CMS reporting, and gets paid only when they get paid. CMS Mandate Timeline — Every Provider Is Affected
NOW
ACCESS Model Applications Open — Cohort 2 Deadline: January 2027
CMS ACCESS Model pays primary care practices monthly per-patient fees for chronic care management across eCKM, CKM, MSK, and behavioral health tracks. 10-year revenue stream per enrolled cohort. Honeycomb Medical (InPursuit partner) submitted Cohort 1. Cohort 2 deadline January 2027. Applications roll through 2033 — 8 more cohorts.
2026
TEAM Model Launches — Mandatory Hospital Participation in Selected CBSAs
Transforming Episode Accountability Model mandates hospital participation in cardiac and surgical episode bundles in selected geographic areas. Zero opt-out. Every affected hospital requires real-time post-acute data exchange across SNF, home health, and rehab partners — that is TETRA Ex's core function.
2026
CMS Medicare App Library Live — InPursuit in All 3 Patient-Facing Categories
140M+ Medicare beneficiaries now access the InPursuit App via Medicare.gov across Conversational AI, Kill the Clipboard, and Chronic Care Management. Government-validated distribution to the exact patient population ACCESS Model serves — at zero acquisition cost to InPursuit.
2027
LEAD Model Launches — CMS Explicitly Targets Small and Rural Practices
CMS LEAD Model is specifically designed for smaller, independent, and rural practices historically shut out of VBC. This is the federal government deliberately expanding the VBC mandate to InPursuit's core market — and signaling that small practice deployment is the next national priority.
2027
MIPS Penalties Increase — FFS Revenue Erodes for Every Non-VBC Provider
MIPS penalty thresholds rise annually. Providers who do not demonstrate quality and interoperability performance face increasing payment reductions. For small practices operating on thin margins, VBC migration ceases to be optional.
2028
ACO REACH Expansion — More Provider Types, Lower Barriers
CMS has signaled expansion of ACO REACH to include additional provider types and geographic regions. By 2028, the addressable pool for InPursuit's ACCESS-as-a-Service model will be materially larger than the current market.
2030
CMS Goal: 100% of Traditional Medicare in Accountable Care
Every remaining fee-for-service provider must have data infrastructure to participate in accountable care. By 2030, InPursuit's objective is to be the established, CMS-validated default. The infrastructure that becomes the standard in this wave will be entrenched for a decade — the way Epic became entrenched in hospitals after Meaningful Use.
The analogy is Meaningful Use. When CMS mandated EHR adoption in 2009, the vendors already deployed became locked-in standards — not necessarily because they were the best available, but because they were there, credentialed, and battle-tested when the mandate hit. Epic, Cerner, and Athenahealth each became generational companies from that single policy wave. The VBC mandate is the biggest transition in US healthcare history, and InPursuit has federal validation, CMS ecosystem approval, a live product, and revenue-aligned deployment. The next four years are the window. We go now.
