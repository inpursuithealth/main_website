How It Works
Two Protections. Equity Upside + Debt Backstop.
A convertible note is a debt instrument that converts to equity upon a qualifying financing event. Your note accrues interest at 5% annually, and the total — principal plus accrued interest — converts at the $25M valuation cap. Here's how both mechanisms protect you.
Cap Conversion

$25M Valuation Cap

Your note converts at the $25M cap regardless of the Series A price. $100K principal + ~$3,750 accrued interest (9 months at 5%) = $103,750 converts at the $25M cap.

$103,750 ÷ $25M = 0.415% ownership

At a $50M Series A, your 0.415% is worth $207,500 on Day 1.

Maturity Protection

18-Month Debt Backstop

If no qualifying financing occurs within 18 months, your principal + accrued interest ($105,000 at maturity) becomes due and payable. This is your safety net.

$100K + $5,000 interest = $105,000 due at maturity

Dual protection: equity upside if things go well + debt backstop if they don't.

🛡

Why a note instead of a SAFE? Unlike a SAFE, a convertible note is a legal debt obligation. If the company doesn't raise a qualifying round before maturity, you have a contractual right to repayment of principal plus accrued interest. This gives note holders a layer of downside protection that SAFE investors don't have — while still providing the same equity conversion upside when things go right.

The Bottom Line

Interest Accrual = Extra Equity. Plus Debt Protection.

Unlike a SAFE, a convertible note gives you both equity upside AND a legal claim to repayment if things don't work out. Your 5% annual interest accrues as extra equity upon conversion — and if no qualifying round occurs within 18 months, your $105,000 (principal + interest) becomes a debt obligation. It's the best of both worlds: startup upside with a safety net.
